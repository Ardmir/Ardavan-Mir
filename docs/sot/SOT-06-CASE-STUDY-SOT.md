## Case study strategy

Use outcome-first, NDA-safe storytelling. Case studies should show senior product judgment, not just screens.

Every case study should answer:

- What was the customer/business problem?
- Why was it complex?
- What was Ardavan’s role?
- How did he structure the ambiguity?
- What design decisions mattered?
- What changed because of the work?
- What is verified vs. unverified?

## Case study title formula

```text
[Outcome or customer value]: Designing [product/workflow/system] for [audience/context]
```

Examples:

```text
From complexity to confidence: Designing IA for enterprise financial workflows
Making advanced accounting legible: Dimensional Chart of Accounts for QuickBooks Online Advanced
Designing trust into intelligent workflows: AI-native experiences for Intuit Enterprise Suite
```

## Case study structure (detailed)

### 1. Outcome-first headline

One sentence that states the value clearly without overclaiming.

### 2. At a glance

- Company / product:
- Timeframe: `[VERIFY]`
- Role: `[VERIFY]`
- Team: `[VERIFY]`
- Platform:
- Constraints / NDA notes:
- Metrics or qualitative signals: `[VERIFY]`

### 3. Context

What was happening in the business, product, or customer journey that made this work important?

### 4. Problem

Who was struggling? What were they trying to do? Why did existing workflows, IA, or system models create friction?

### 5. Role and scope

Be specific. Choose verbs carefully:

- Designed
- Shaped
- Contributed to
- Partnered on
- Facilitated
- Prototyped
- Mapped
- Synthesized

Use “led” or “owned” only if verified.

### 6. Constraints

Include constraints that show senior judgment:

- Enterprise complexity.
- Accounting/financial accuracy.
- Trust and compliance expectations.
- AI explainability.
- Multi-team dependencies.
- Technical constraints.
- NDA constraints.

### 7. Process

Use 4–6 concise subsections:

- Discovery and customer evidence.
- Decision journey / workflow map.
- Information architecture model.
- AI experience model.
- Concepts explored.
- Prototype and feedback.
- System / design-system decisions.
- Cross-functional alignment.

### 8. Key decisions and tradeoffs

Document what was chosen, what was rejected, and why — especially IA and AI trust patterns.

### 9. AI experience design (where relevant)

Explainability, review, control, recovery, and human judgment boundaries.

### 10. Solution

Show final or representative experience through:

- Blurred or abstracted screens.
- IA diagrams.
- Workflow diagrams.
- Before/after structural diagrams.
- Annotated UI snippets.
- Decision model visuals.

### 11. Evidence and artifacts

See P1-05 artifact inventory. Classify every asset before publication.

### 12. Impact

Use verified numbers first. If unavailable, use credible qualitative outcomes.

Potential metrics to verify:

- Adoption.
- Engagement.
- Setup completion.
- Error reduction.
- Time saved.
- Comprehension improvement.
- Support reduction.
- Stakeholder confidence.
- Launch milestone.

Never invent these.

### 13. Reflection

End with senior-level reflection:

- What tradeoffs mattered?
- What changed in your design practice?
- What would you measure next?
- What does this project reveal about how you design intelligent systems?

## NDA-safe storytelling techniques

- Show structure instead of confidential screens.
- Use abstracted interface diagrams.
- Blur or recreate sensitive UI.
- Focus on problem framing, decision process, and design rationale.
- Avoid internal product names if not public.
- Avoid unreleased feature details.
